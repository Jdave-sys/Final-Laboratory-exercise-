Sinuglaw

Where the Smoky Grill Meets the Zesty Sea

The Story Behind the Dish

In the bustling markets of Mindanao, the aroma of charcoal-grilled pork belly (Sinugba) mingles with the sharp, clean scent of vinegar-cured fish (Kinilaw). Sinuglaw is a celebration of contrast: the heat of the fire and the coolness of the ocean, bound together by spicy ginger and tart calamansi.

Ingredients

  • The Sinugba: 500g Grilled Pork Belly, sliced.
  • The Kinilaw: 500g Fresh Tuna or Tanigue, cubed.
  • The Marinade: Coconut vinegar, calamansi juice, ginger, and bird's eye chili.
  • The Crunch: Red onions, cucumber, and green mango.

Preparation

  1. Marinate the fish in vinegar for 10 minutes, then drain.
  2. Toss with fresh vinegar, aromatics, and spices.
  3. Add the smoky grilled pork just before serving to maintain the texture.
